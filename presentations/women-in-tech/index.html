The statistics used in this presentation have come from a number of different sources and range from a number of different dates. As with all statistics, these may not be an exactly right, and things may have changed slightly since the time that the articles I took them from were written.

The problem

  • Around 14% of employees in the UK Tech Industry are female
  • Lack of diversity
  • Missing out on talent

Facts and statistics

  • 31% of Facebook's employees are female
  • 39% of LinkedIn's employees are female
  • 30% of Google's employees are female
    • But only 17% in 'tech' roles
  • 30% of Apple's employees are female
    • But only 20% in 'tech' roles
  • 30% of Twitter's employees are female
    • But only 10% in 'tech' roles
  • 18% of Coderus' employees are female
    • But only 9% in 'tech' roles

Facts and statistics

  • In 2012, women gained 57% of all undergraduate degrees
    • And, women gained 52% of all science and maths undergraduate degrees
    • Yet, women gained only 18% of computer and information science degrees
  • Martha Lane-Fox stated at a talk last week that "it is not misleading to suggest that 98% of code that internet and web technologies rely on was programmed by men."

Facts and statistics

  • In the Hour of Code's first week, 15 million students took part, over half of which were female
  • The University of California, Berkeley, had more women than men registered for their Computer Science course in Spring 2014 (106 to 104)
    • The module called "Introduction to Symbolic Programming" was renamed to "Beauty and the Joy of Computing"
    • At the same time, female enrolment on the course increased by 50%

Why is this important?

  • Strong female mentors can help improve professional experience
  • A diverse workforce leads to better products and results
  • Helps to reduce discrimination
  • Helps to encourage more women into tech
  • Leads to better innovation in sub-industries

What can Coderus do?

  • Become more attractive to women
    • Only one woman on the website, in the background
  • Put in place diversity goals
    • Think about these when hiring
    • Think about these when promoting
  • Promote Women in Tech
    • Attend local schools to talk about Women in Tech
    • Work with local businesses
